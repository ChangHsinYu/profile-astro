        I once came across a video about visual eavesdropping using laser microphones, and realized that this technology opened up a new path in my research into sound signals. Although the technology itself is not particularly new, unlike other forms of wireless transmission, its mechanism—using photosensitive components to receive fluctuations in light and directly amplify the resulting signal—allows me to intervene in the process of signal transmission within visible, physical space. Because light serves as the carrier of the signal, it also creates an ambiguous space between the visible and the invisible: we can see the light, yet cannot perceive its rapid fluctuations in brightness; we can hear sound transmitted through light, yet see no physical wires carrying it.
        
        In this series, light functions neither as illumination nor as image, but as a carrier of information. I am interested in how signals can be carried through light, and how detection and conversion can bring otherwise imperceptible variations into the range of human perception. Light is usually understood as a medium through which we see the world, but could it also become an entry point into another mode of perception? When fluctuations in light are converted and amplified into sound, we temporarily gain access to a mode of perception beyond ordinary sensory experience. Through these works, I attempt to reconfigure the boundary between seeing and listening, allowing light not only to be seen, but also to be “heard.”
        
        *Listen to the Light series (2022-2026)* begins with the rapid flickering of everyday light sources that is imperceptible to the naked eye. I modulate different sound signals into LED light sources installed throughout the exhibition space and construct a detection device that uses a solar panel as its receiving element, converting subtle variations in light intensity into electrical signals and directly amplifying them as sound. As viewers move through the bright and seemingly quiet space, the detector allows them to receive sounds hidden within different sources of light. For me, “detection” in this work is not about searching for something truly unknowable, but about revealing signals that have always existed within our everyday environment yet remain unnoticed because of the limits of our perception.

        It is precisely this condition of “existing yet remaining directly imperceptible” that has gradually made detection another central concern of this series. The invisible does not necessarily hide where we cannot see; it may also be concealed within the very things we are already looking at. Some signals undoubtedly exist but lie beyond the range of human senses, becoming perceptible only through instruments and processes of conversion. Other forms of existence, however, cannot even be confirmed as real; their possibility can only be inferred from accidental traces and inexplicable signs. I am interested both in phenomena that cannot be directly perceived and in forms of existence that can only be inferred through their traces—whether the flickering within light, barely perceptible low-frequency vibrations, unknown phenomena, mysterious creatures, or signals that cannot be verified. They allow the unknown to remain suspended between reality and imagination.
        
        *Myst series (2023-2026)*, by contrast, attempts to push detection toward another kind of unverifiable existence. I am interested in the signs that precede the appearance of mysterious creatures in film and other visual media: rather than directly revealing the body of a monster, vibrations, sounds, smells, and environmental anomalies often sustain our imagination of the unknown for longer. In the work, water mist suspended in the air interferes with a laser beam, causing a photosensitive component to receive a continuously fluctuating signal. The signal is amplified into low-frequency noise and transmitted through vibration speakers mounted on transparent corrugated panels, causing the entire space to vibrate. Viewers never encounter a visible creature; they can only infer the possibility of some presence through low rumbles, vibrations, and mist, leaving the work suspended in a state in which “something might be there” but can never be confirmed.

        In *Listen to the Light series (2022-2026)*, detection brings signals that already exist but cannot be directly perceived into the range of human perception. In *Myst series (2023-2026)*, however, detection does not lead to a definitive answer; instead, sound, vibration, and mist leave behind further traces of the unknown. For me, detection therefore does not always mean revelation. It can make the unknown perceptible, expand the boundaries of what we are able to sense, and even generate further unknowns beyond those newly extended boundaries.
        
        Our imagination of the unknown is inevitably grounded in existing scientific, cultural, and sensory experience. When we attempt to describe forms of existence that remain beyond our understanding, we simultaneously expose the limits of our own knowledge. Yet the continuous imagination and exploration of the unknown repeatedly reshape those limits, allowing what was once incomprehensible to gradually enter the realm of the known, while producing new unknowns further beyond. We may never truly reach that distant frontier of the unknown, but for me, to continue imagining, approaching, and probing it is itself a way of confronting the unknown—and perhaps also my own way of resisting nothingness.

        有一次無意間看到關於雷射間諜麥克風（Laser Microphone）視覺竊聽技術的影片，我發覺這項技術為我對聲音訊號的研究開拓了一條新的路徑。儘管它並不是一項新穎的技術，但不同於其他無線傳輸方式，透過光敏元件接收光的波動並將其訊號直接放大的機制，讓我得以在可見的物理空間中對訊號的傳輸過程進行干擾。也因為訊號以光作為載體，它創造了一個介於可見與不可見之間的曖昧空間：我們看得見光，卻無法辨識其中快速的明暗變化；我們透過光聽見聲音，卻看不見任何實體線材的傳輸。 在這個系列中，光不作為照明工具或影像，而是一種訊息的載體。我關注訊號如何透過光被攜帶，以及如何透過探測與轉換，使其中原本無法被直接感知的變化進入人的感知範圍。光通常被視為觀看世界的媒介，然而，它是否也能成為另一種感知的入口？當光的波動被轉換並放大為聲音，人們便暫時獲得了一種超出日常經驗的感知方式。我試圖透過作品重新配置觀看與聆聽的界線，使光不再只是被看見，而是能夠被「聽見」。
        
        *Listen to the Light series (2022-2026)* 從日常光源中肉眼無法辨識的快速閃爍出發。我將不同的聲音訊號調製於展場中的 LED 光源，並製作一個以太陽能板作為接收元件的探測裝置，將光強度的微小變化轉換為電訊號並直接放大為聲音。觀眾在明亮而看似寧靜的空間中移動時，能透過探測器接收到隱藏於不同光源中的聲音。對我而言，作品中的「探測」並不是尋找某個真正不可知的存在，而是揭露那些始終存在於日常環境之中、卻因感知限制而被忽略的訊號。

        正是這種「存在卻無法被直接感知」的狀態，使「探測」逐漸成為這個系列的另一個核心。不可見的東西不一定隱藏在我們看不見的地方，它也可能隱藏在我們正在觀看的事物之中。某些訊號確實存在，卻超出了人的感官範圍，只能透過儀器與轉換被察覺；而另一些存在，我們甚至無法確定它們是否真實，只能從偶然出現的痕跡與難以解釋的徵兆中推測其可能性。我對這些無法被直接感知的現象，以及只能透過痕跡被推測的存在感興趣，無論是光中的閃爍、若有似無的低頻振動、未知現象、神秘生物，甚至無法被證實的訊號，它們使未知得以停留在真實與想像之間。

        *Myst series (2023-2026)* 試圖將探測推向另一種無法被證實的存在。我對影視作品中神秘生物現身前的徵兆感興趣：與其直接呈現怪物的形體，震動、聲音、氣味與環境中的異常往往更能延長對未知的想像。作品利用空氣中的水霧干擾雷射光，使光敏元件接收到持續波動的訊號，經過放大後轉化為低頻噪音，並透過安裝於透明浪板上的震動喇叭使整個空間產生震動。觀眾始終無法看見任何具體的生物，只能透過低鳴、振動與霧氣推測某種存在的可能，使作品停留在「某物或許存在」而尚未被證實的狀態

        在 *Listen to the Light series (2022-2026)* 中，探測使原本存在卻無法被直接感知的訊號進入人的感知範圍；而在 *Myst series (2023-2026)* 中，探測並未指向一個確定的答案，反而透過聲音、震動與霧氣留下更多關於未知的痕跡。對我而言，探測因此並不總是意味著揭露，它既可能使未知變得可感，也可能拓展我們感知的邊界，甚至在這條新的邊界之外產生更多未知。我們對未知的想像往往建立在既有的科學、文化與感知經驗之上，因此，當我們試圖描述那些尚未被理解的存在時，也同時暴露了自身認知的邊界。然而，正是對未知持續不斷的想像與探測，又一次次改變了這條邊界，使原本無法理解的事物逐漸進入已知的範圍，並在更遠的地方產生新的未知。我們或許永遠無法真正抵達那個未知的遠方，但對我而言，持續地想像、接近與探測它，本身就是一種面對未知的方式，甚至也是我對抗虛無的一種方法。
